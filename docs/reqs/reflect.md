# Reflect

> Grow Module | Performance System | Two-Way Conversation | Employee View | Manager View | HR / People Ops View

## Overview

Reflect is the structured two-way conversation feature designed to maintain the working relationship between employee and manager so that goals, Check-ins, coaching, and performance conversations can function as intended.

Reflect is not a performance conversation. It is a relationship-centered conversation that examines support, feedback, requests, and mutual commitments through a structured process. The feature is development data only and is never used in formal performance evaluations.

### Design Principles

- [ ] Reflect is relationship-centered, not evaluative — **NOT IMPLEMENTED (#31)**
- [ ] Employee and manager prepare independently and enter with equal structural voice — **NOT IMPLEMENTED (#31)**
- [ ] Conversation begins with a shared map rather than either party's verbal agenda — **NOT IMPLEMENTED (#31)**
- [ ] Leadership Library guidance and company MVV shape preparation, conduct, and follow-through — **NOT IMPLEMENTED (#31)**
- [ ] Reflect data remains private except for defined aggregate and safety-related routing rules — **NOT IMPLEMENTED (#31)**
- [ ] A Reflect without mutual commitment is treated as an incomplete outcome — **NOT IMPLEMENTED (#31)**

### Reflect Lifecycle

| Phase | Timing | Who Acts | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Preparation | 48 hours before | Manager and Employee independently | Prepare honest input across working relationship dimensions |
| Conversation | Scheduled Reflect time | Employee and Manager together | Use the map, exchange feedback, make mutual commitments |
| Post-Reflect | Within 24 hours after | Manager and Employee independently | Capture how conversation landed and seed next actions |
| Triggers and Routing | Continuous | System, Employee, Manager, HR/People Ops | Surface Reflect when relationship needs deeper attention |

## Manager View

### Preparation (48 hours before, completed independently)

- [ ] **MR-1: Relationship Quality Reflection** — "How would you describe the quality of your working relationship with this employee right now — not their performance, but the relationship itself? Where does it feel strong? Where does it feel like there is distance or missed connection?" Compared against ER-1. — **NOT IMPLEMENTED (#31)**
- [ ] **MR-2: Manager Self-Assessment** — "In what specific ways do you feel you are showing up well as this employee's manager? Where do you feel you are falling short or where could your support be stronger?" Compared against ER-2 upward feedback. — **NOT IMPLEMENTED (#31)**
- [ ] **MR-3: Things Left Unsaid** — "Is there anything you have been holding back in your Check-ins with this employee — a concern, a piece of feedback, an appreciation you have not named, or something about the working dynamic that feels unresolved?" Private to manager. Never surfaced in conversation map. — **NOT IMPLEMENTED (#31)**
- [ ] **MR-4: What I Need from This Employee** — "If you could name one thing this employee could do that would make this working relationship more effective, what would it be? Frame it as a request, not a criticism." Compared against ER-4 to create request exchange. — **NOT IMPLEMENTED (#31)**

### Conversation (6 structured moves)

- [ ] **Move 1: Open with the Conversation Map** — both parties view the map together before either speaks; map displays alignment and divergence across working relationship dimensions without blame — **NOT IMPLEMENTED (#31)**
- [ ] **Move 2: Name What Is Working** — acknowledge strengths before addressing what needs attention; recognition is specific and genuine — **NOT IMPLEMENTED (#31)**
- [ ] **Move 3: Employee Delivers Upward Feedback** — employee perspective on manager effectiveness comes first; manager expected to receive without defensiveness; structure prevents employee calibration
- [ ] **Move 4: Manager Delivers Feedback to Employee** — manager shares observations using SBI framework (Situation, Behavior, Impact); Leadership Library guidance reminds manager to avoid character judgment
- [ ] **Move 5: Exchange Requests** — both parties share what they need the other to do differently; requests framed as invitations with reasons, not demands
- [ ] **Move 6: Mutual Commitment** — both parties name one concrete change before conversation ends; commitments are observable, logged, and seeded into future Check-ins

### Post-Reflect (within 24 hours)

- [ ] **MPR-1: Employee Experience of Being Heard** — "Did the employee leave feeling genuinely understood?" Compared against EPR-1. Persistent divergence triggers a relationship quality signal.
- [ ] **MPR-2: Learning from Upward Feedback** — "What did the employee feedback teach the manager?" Becomes part of manager development record within Leadership Library.
- [ ] **MPR-3: Manager Commitment** — name the specific commitment made in Move 6; appears in pre-Check-in context brief until employee confirms it has been honored
- [ ] **MPR-4: Next Reflect Timing** — "When should the next Reflect be scheduled?" Compared against EPR-4. The earlier date always wins.

## Employee View

> The employee prepares honestly, gives upward feedback first, participates in mutual commitments, and can request a Reflect at any time without explanation.

### Preparation (48 hours before, completed independently)

- [ ] **ER-1: Relationship Quality Reflection** — "How would you describe the quality of your working relationship with your manager right now — not their technical skills, but the relationship itself? Where does it feel strong? Where does it feel like something is missing or unclear?" Compared against MR-1.
- [ ] **ER-2: Upward Feedback** — "In what ways does your manager show up well for you? Where do you wish their support looked different?" Compared against MR-2.
- [ ] **ER-3: Things Left Unsaid** — "Is there something you have wanted to raise with your manager but have not felt the right moment?" Private to employee. Never surfaced in conversation map.
- [ ] **ER-4: What I Need from My Manager** — "If you could name one thing your manager could do that would make this working relationship more effective for you, what would it be? Frame it as a request with a reason." Compared against MR-4.

### Conversation (6 structured moves)

- [ ] **Move 1: The Conversation Map Opens** — both parties view map together before either speaks; map is a starting point for conversation, not a verdict
- [ ] **Move 2: Name What Is Working** — employee shares appreciation for what is working; signals good faith and supports psychological safety
- [ ] **Move 3: Deliver Upward Feedback** — employee perspective comes before manager feedback; employee encouraged to use specific observations and focus on needed support
- [ ] **Move 4: Receive Feedback from Manager** — listen, reflect back, then respond; prompt support includes: "What I heard you say is..." to confirm understanding
- [ ] **Move 5: Name Your Request** — share what you need your manager to do differently; requests are concrete, reasoned, and confirmable
- [ ] **Move 6: Make a Mutual Commitment** — employee names one concrete change before conversation ends; commitment appears in future Check-in reflections until honored

### Post-Reflect (within 24 hours)

- [ ] **EPR-1: Did I Feel Heard?** — "Did the employee leave feeling genuinely understood?" Compared against MPR-1. This is the most important post-Reflect signal.
- [ ] **EPR-2: Did I Say What I Needed to Say?** — "Did the conversation create enough safety for honesty?" If significant content held back, a private signal can route into manager's next Reflect preparation.
- [ ] **EPR-3: Your Commitment** — name the specific commitment made in Move 6; appears in employee next pre-Check-in reflection until honored
- [ ] **EPR-4: Next Reflect Timing** — "When should the next Reflect happen?" Compared against MPR-4. The earlier date always wins.

## HR / People Ops View

> Organizational visibility into Reflect only at aggregate and workflow level. Raw content must remain protected so trust in the feature is preserved.

- [ ] **Conversation Content Privacy** — HR does not see raw Reflect preparation, conversation content, or post-Reflect reflections; development data only; HR sees content only if a defined safety escalation threshold requires organizational intervention
- [ ] **Trigger Status Visibility** — HR can see when Reflect has been triggered and whether it is pending, scheduled, completed, or overdue; supports operational follow-through without exposing substance
- [ ] **Aggregate Pattern Visibility** — HR can see pattern-level signals related to Reflect frequency, completion, and recurring safety concerns; surfaced through Canopy without exposing verbatim content
- [ ] **Leadership Library Support Visibility** — HR can see when Leadership Library content is being surfaced to managers in response to recurring development or safety patterns
- [ ] **Escalation Governance** — HR can intervene only when thresholds are met or existing company policy requires action; Reflect should never become a shadow performance file; intervention tied to safety, repeated unresolved issues, or defined company escalation rules

---

## Conversation Map Requirements

- [ ] **Shared Opening Artifact** — map appears at opening of Reflect before either person begins verbally framing the conversation; prevents agenda control by either party
- [ ] **Dimensions Included** — map displays alignment and divergence across relationship quality, support, and request-related dimensions; divergence framed as opportunity to reconnect, not failure
- [ ] **No Private Items Surfaced** — private prompts (things left unsaid) do not appear in the map; these exist to help each party prepare for honesty, not expose unfinished thoughts
- [ ] **Neutral Framing** — map language must remain non-blaming and non-evaluative; designed to create openness, not defensiveness

---

## Triggers and Routing

- [ ] **Trigger 1: Persistent Check-in Gap Signal** — gap signals in two consecutive Check-in cycles trigger a Reflect suggestion; same dimension persisting across cycles means the issue needs deeper conversation
- [ ] **Trigger 2: Manual Request by Either Party** — either party can request a Reflect at any time without explanation; single action notifies the other party; receiving party confirms time within defined response window
- [ ] **Trigger 3: Lifecycle Milestone** — Reflect automatically suggested at key points: 90 days into new role or new manager relationship, midpoint of performance period, activation of new goal set, after coaching or corrective action conversations
- [ ] **Trigger 4: Quarterly Minimum** — Reflect occurs at minimum once every 90 days; if none within 90 days, system sends soft prompt to both parties
- [ ] **Routing Principle: Development Data Only** — Reflect data is never used in formal performance evaluations; no Reflect summary included in performance record; honest participation depends on this boundary

---

## Cross-System Linkages

- [ ] **Check-in to Reflect** — persistent Check-in gaps can route the relationship into Reflect; addresses what regular Check-in cadence cannot resolve
- [ ] **Reflect to Check-in** — mutual commitments from Reflect seed back into future Check-ins; manager commitments appear in context brief; employee commitments appear in future reflection prompts
- [ ] **Reflect to Leadership Library** — post-Reflect patterns can trigger Leadership Library content surfacing; managers receive timely development support based on emerging patterns
- [ ] **Reflect to Canopy** — aggregate, threshold-based signals can surface to HR/People Ops; visibility limited to organizational support and governance needs

## Data Privacy and Trust Architecture

| Data Element | Employee Sees | Manager Sees | HR / People Ops Sees |
|---|---|---|---|
| Employee Preparation Responses | Own responses only | Nothing before map opens | Nothing |
| Manager Preparation Responses | Nothing before map opens | Own responses only | Nothing |
| Conversation Map | Visible during Reflect | Visible during Reflect | Nothing |
| Manager Post-Reflect Responses | Nothing | Own responses only | Nothing unless escalation threshold crossed |
| Employee Post-Reflect Responses | Own responses only | Nothing | Nothing unless escalation threshold crossed |
| Shared Commitments | Visible | Visible | Status only when required for workflow visibility |
| Trigger Status | Visible when triggered | Visible when triggered | Visible at workflow level |

## Operational Timing Requirements

- [ ] **Preparation Delivery** — delivered 48 hours before Reflect; manager and employee prepare independently
- [ ] **Conversation Availability** — scheduled once both parties accept or confirm conversation timing; manual requests require acknowledgment within configured window
- [ ] **Post-Reflect Notification** — sent within one hour of scheduled end time; reminder at 12 hours if incomplete; window closes at 24 hours
- [ ] **Next Reflect Timing Rule** — the earlier of manager and employee recommended dates wins; prevents hard conversations from being deferred indefinitely
- [ ] **Completion Status Tracking** — Prepare, Conversation, and Post-Reflect completion states recorded; supports system continuity and limited HR workflow visibility without exposing content
